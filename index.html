Introduction

I am a theoretical linguist currently working as a “Hundred Talents Program” researcher at Zhejiang University. My job also involves teaching and graduate student advising. Before I took up my current position in 2020, I had studied at University of Cambridge (Gonville and Caius College) and received my PhD degree in 2019.

Research

My main research area is formal syntax, with an empirical focus on Chinese grammar. I do both synchronic and diachronic research and am particularly interested in understudied Chinese varieties. A dialect that I have studied in depth is Dongying Mandarin, which is a Ji Lu Mandarin subvariety spoken in the Yellow River Delta area.

Beyong Chinese, I am also interested in detailed crosslinguistic comparison. Languages I have looked into include Hungarian, German, Japanese, Korean, etc. Below are some specific topics that I have investigated:

  • Complex/compound word formation
  • Aspects and event structure
  • Semifunctional vocabulary items
  • Sentence-final particles

And some big-picture issues I have worked on include:

  • Foundations of syntactic categories
  • Linguistic features and their logic
  • Category theory and its application in linguistics
  • Noncore linguistic phenomena (e.g., emotion, register) and their syntacticization

The serious attention I have paid to word-internal structure makes my expertise lie at the syntax-morphology interface. And my amateur interest in mathematical logic has extended my curiosity to the syntax-semantics interface too. So, my overarching enthusiasm is in formally rigorous and logically consistent analyses of language.

Beyond my main research domains, I am also interested in the following language-related topics:

  • Internet language (e.g., emojis)
  • Writing systems (especially Chinese characters)
  • Application of linguistics in AI
  • Linguistically aided language learning

Morphological analyzer

I attended the 2016 San Sebastián summer school “Codefest” on NLP and developed a morphological analyzer for Hungarian, which can automatically analyze the morphosemantic components of inflected words as well as generate orthographically correct words based on abstract linguistic representations.

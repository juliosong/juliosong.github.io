Introduction

I am a theoretical linguist currently working as a “Hundred Talents Program” researcher at Zhejiang University (Hangzhou, China). Before taking up my current position (May 2020), I studied at University of Cambridge (Gonville & Caius College), where I received my PhD degree in Linguistics (Oct. 2019). Before Cambridge, I had studied at Beijing Foreign Studies University (majoring in Hungarian). I am curious about all aspects of language and am particularly fascinated by its syntactic module. I mainly work within the theoretical framework of Noam Chomsky's transformational generative grammar but am open-minded about other frameworks too. I enjoy learning new perspectives and am always happy to observe how different theories converge. I also actively explore interdisciplinary directions in my research and endeavor to seek common ground across paradigms.

Research

My main research area is formal syntax. I do both synchronic and diachronic research and am particularly interested in understudied Chinese varieties, many of which are also endangered. A variety that I have studied in depth is Dongying Mandarin, which is a Ji Lu Mandarin subvariety spoken in the Yellow River Delta area (northwestern Shandong Province).

Beyond Chinese, I am interested in detailed crosslinguistic comparison. Languages I have previously looked into include Hungarian, German, Japanese, Korean, etc. More recently, I have become interested in polysynthetic languages as well, especially in their “lexical affixes.” Below are some specific phenomena that I have investigated:

  • Complex/compound word formation
  • Aspects and event structure
  • Semifunctional/semilexical vocabulary items
  • Sentence-final particles

Aside from empirical studies, my research also involves a highly conceptual and metatheoretical aspect. Below are some more abstract issues I have worked on:

  • Foundations of syntactic categories
  • Linguistic features and their logic
  • Category theory and its application in linguistics

Overall, my academic enthusiasm lies in formally rigorous and logically consistent analysis of human language. Outside of my main research domains, I also like thinking about the following language-related topics:

  • Internet language (e.g., emojis, novel usages)
  • Writing systems (esp. Chinese characters)
  • Application of linguistics in AI
  • Linguistically aided language learning

Projects

In progress

  • Categorical linguistics (linguistics + mathematics), since 2019
    Applying category theory to theoretical linguistics (esp. generative grammar), with a focus on the big picture and general directions; currently attempting to combine the monoidal category approach and the monad approach in syntactic/semantic theory [preliminary result]
  • Telicization in Chinese dialects, since 2020
    Examining ways of telicization (esp. in the verbal domain) across Chinese varieties; currently focusing on Dongying Mandarin and Wenzhou Wu (joint work with Ke Javena Wu) [preliminary result]
  • Noncanonical pronouns, since 2021
    Investigating pronominal items that cannot be easily put in established categories (e.g., textbook pronouns, imposters); currently focusing on Chinese, Vietnamese, and Afrikaans (joint work with Li Nguyen and Theresa Biberauer) [preliminary result]
  • Typology of semilexicality, since 2021
    Surveying different types of semilexical elements across a wide range of languages and attempting to provide a unified account within the framework of root syntax [preliminary result]
  • Multiparty session types (computer science), since 2022
    Exploring the concurrent semantics of multiparty session types and their relation to separation logic, with a particular interest in the category-theoretic perspective (joint work with Francisco Ferreira)
